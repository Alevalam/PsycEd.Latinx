Addressing Mental Health

Stigma in Latinx Communities

A Parent Intervention

OBJECTIVES


  1. Educate about mental health in general
  2. Educate about stigma (Self Stigma and Perceived Stigma)
  3. Increase likelihood of people seeking resources and giving access to resources (Talk about self-reliance)


Program Overview


DURATION

  • 6 weeks of bi-weekly sessions
  • 4 hour sessions

LOVE

JOB DONE



POPULATION

  • Latinx parents of middle schoolers
  • Pomona, CA

CERTIFIED

HARD WORK

WHY THIS MATTERS


  • Middle school is a time when mental health issues start to come up
  • Lack of comprehensive culturally specific programs
  • Stigma towards mental health in the Latinx community

CULTURAL FACTORS TO CONSIDER


  • Importance of Respect
  • Religious Affiliations
  • Familismo
  • Machismo

PURPOSE OF INTEGRATED PSYCHOEDUCATION

RECRUITMENT AND INCENTIVES


  • Flyers in community centers, churches, schools
  • Incentives:
    • Childcare
    • Breakfast and lunch: Ethnic foods from community vendors
    • Technology: Android tablet to be used throughout the sessions to access resources on website and will be kept by participants after completion of the program (One per family)
